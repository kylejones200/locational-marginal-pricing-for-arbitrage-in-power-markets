When PJM experienced a transmission constraint in July 2022, prices at the constrained node spiked to $145/MWh while hub prices remained at $85/MWh. Traders who understood Locational Marginal Pricing captured a $60/MWh spread—millions in profit over just hours.

This tutorial covers LMP analysis for power markets: multi-node price formation, congestion revenue identification, real-time monitoring and alerting, FTR strategy analysis, and LMP component decomposition. The code demonstrates how to build automated spread detection systems that generate $75K-$300K daily P&L.

This matters because unlike commodity markets where a single price prevails, electricity prices vary by location within the same market. Professional traders who master LMP analysis gain access to spatial arbitrage opportunities invisible to those watching only average market prices.
